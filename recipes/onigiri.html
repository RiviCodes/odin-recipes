Onigiri

Japanese rice balls, stuff the with anything you like

This recipe is delicious, takes 40 minutes to 1 hour to be prepared.

Also, you can stuff them with any incredient (i.e beef, pork, shrimp) you like!

Ingredients:

  • 2/3 cup of short-grain rice (rinsed)
  • 2/3 cup of water
  • 3 sheets of nori (aka dry seaweed) cut into 1-inch strips
  • Salt (according to your liking)
  • Seasoned sesame seeds (to your taste)
  • Shiso with hijiki (dried seaweed flakes) to taste

Steps:

  1. Combine rice and water in a heavy-bottomed pot with a tight-fitting lid.
  2. Soak for 20 to 30 minutes.
  3. Cover and bring to a boil over medium heat.
  4. Once water is boiling, reduce heat to low and cook until water is completely absorbed, 12 to 13 minutes.
  5. Remove from the heat; keep covered and steam for another 10 minutes.
  6. Remove lid and cool to room temperature, about 20 minutes.
  7. Wet your hands and form small amounts of rice into triangle shapes if possible, or round balls.
  8. Wrap each onigiri with a strip of nori.
  9. Season with salt and garnish with sesame seeds and seaweed flakes.
